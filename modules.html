Tempinator_Dependence

This module is a dynamic 8-step tempo sequencer where each step's tempo can depend upon a previous step's tempo. In other words, it implements metric modulation.

For the purposes of understanding tempo relationships, think of the following: the duration of x beats of the new tempo is the same as y beats of the dependence tempo.

Knobs

  • (1) BPM – Sets the master tempo of the module. It is the same for each step

  • (9) Beats of Dependent Tempo – Sets the number of dependence tempo beats

  • (A) Beats of New Tempo – Sets the number of new tempo beats

  • (C) Hold – Sets the amount of beats made before moving to the next step

  • (D) Rate – Fine tunes the speed of the tempo

Lights

  • (3) Sequence Steps – Indicate the output step, active steps, and innactive steps

    • Output Step: bright green light

    • Active Step: dim green light

    • Innactive Step: off light

  • (4) Edit Step – Indicate which step's values are being edited

  • (5) On/Off – Indicate the state of the edit step

Buttons

  • (6) Left – Move edit step to the left

  • (7) On/Off – Turn edit step on or off

  • (8) Right – Move edit step to the right

  • (B) Dependence Selector – Choose what beats the new tempo depends on

    • BPM: Depends on master tempo

    • <: Depends on previous tempo, even if inactive

    • <<: Depends on previous previous tempo, even if inactive

  • (F) Reset – resets sequence to first step

Outputs

  • (2) BPM – Outputs clock pulse of master tempo

  • (G) Clock – Outputs clock pulse of sequence steps

WaitGate

This module is a pulse-programmable gate. The user can adjust the amount of time the gate stays closed and open based on a specified number of pulses.

Inputs

  • (1) Pulse – A clock pulse that the gate uses to determine wait time. Any signal can be used, but pulses are only counted at 10V strength

  • (3) In – Signal to be passed through an open gate

Knobs

  • (4) Closed Wait – Amount of pulses the gate remains closed for

  • (6) Open Wait – Amount of pulses the gate remains open for. Setting this knob to zero means the gate will never close once opened

Lights

  • (2) Closed – Indicates the gate is closed

  • (5) Open – Indicates the gate is open

Output

  • (7) Out – Outputs In signal when gate is open

WaitGate_Zero

This module is a modification of WaitGate. There is no input pulse. Instead, the gate wait times depend on the In signal having a strength of 0V. This alows for the gate to be much more dynamic.

Input

  • (3) In – Signal to be passed through an open gate. Moreover, wait times depend on the signal having a strength of 0V

Knobs

  • (4) Closed Wait – Amount of pulses the gate remains closed for

  • (6) Open Wait – Amount of pulses the gate remains open for. Setting this knob to zero means the gate will never close once opened

Lights

  • (1) Zero – Indicates when the In signal has a strength of 0V

  • (2) Closed – Indicates the gate is closed

  • (5) Open – Indicates the gate is open

Output

  • (7) Out – Outputs In signal when gate is open

WaitGate_Reset

This module is a modification of WaitGate. It implements three reset options and wait indication lights.

Inputs

  • (1) Pulse – A clock pulse that the gate uses to determine wait time. Any signal can be used, but pulses are only counted at 10V strength

  • (2) Master Reset – Resets gate to closed and wait time to zero.

  • (4) In – Signal to be passed through an open gate.

  • (5) Closed Reset – Resets closed wait time to zero if gate is closed.

  • (A) Open Reset – Resets open wait time to zero if gate is open.

Knobs

  • (6) Closed Wait – Amount of pulses the gate remains closed for

  • (9) Open Wait – Amount of pulses the gate remains open for. Setting this knob to zero means the gate will never close once opened

Lights

  • (3) Closed – Indicates the gate is closed

  • (7) Closed Wait Indication – The brightness level corresponds with how close the gate is to opening

  • (8) Open – Indicates the gate is open

  • (C) Open Wait Indication – The brightness level corresponds with how close the gate is to closing

Output

  • (B) Out – Outputs In signal when gate is open

FlipFlop

This module is a multi-trigger flipflop.

Inputs

  • (3) In – Signal to be flipped or flopped

  • (4-7) Triggers – Signals to change state of flipflop when they have a strength of 10V

Lights

  • (2) Flip – Indicates when the Flip state is active

  • (9) Flop – Indicates when the Flop state is active

Outputs

  • (1) Flip Out – Outputs In signal when Flip state is active

  • (8) Flop Out – Outputs In signal when Flop state is active
